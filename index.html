If you were a knight on a chessboard, how long would you survive ?

April 13, 2019 - By Kevin LAM

Well, you will not live for long... 24%, it is the odds for survival of a knight on a typical play. It means that almost 1 knight among 4 survive a chess game. What about the other pieces ?

Average Survival Rates

♕ ♖ ♗ ♘ ♙
Queen Rook Bishop Knight Pawn
39% 48% 32% 24% 49%

Beware because it's an average. For example, central pawns tend to die quickly and side pawns are rather safe.

Why do we care about pieces survival ?

You may wonder why do we care about the survival of our pieces ? After all, the goal is to win the game and you don't have to hesitate to sacrifice some pawns to gain advantages. As in the saying : "you can't make an omelette without breaking eggs" !

However, there is one case that this analysis may be really usefull. Do you remember the wizards' chess scene in the first Harry Potter book ?

This topic was debated on Quora and I will quote Mac Tan :

"This literally was a life-and-death scenario for them [...]. So what pieces did Harry, Ron, and Hermione choose to play as?" "Conceivably Ron could have stood on top of one of the rooks, although that doesn't look as heroic when you're throwing yourself in front of a queen for your friend to deliver a mate."

Then I ask you :

If you were Ron, which piece would you choose and what strategy would you use to survive ?

Please, pick a piece and let's see what happen...

When I will die ?

Pawns are sacrified early-game, rooks are the last rempart !

All pieces have an increasing, then decreasing death chance over time.

You can compare the death probability over time between piece by clicking on show all. You will notice that Pawns die firts, then knights, bishops, queens and finally rooks are the last rempart.

Where I will die ?

The center is a real bloodbath : chess is the real battle for Middle-Earth !

We can see that at the center, every pieces have a high risk to die. That's because in Chess, the occupation of the center is really important : controlling the center gives you a big tactical advantage. You unlock the potential of your pieces while hindering the movements of your oppenent. It explain why players send troops to the center to keep control of the area. And as expected, it will cause a lot of casualties. If I have an advice for you dear fellow chess pieces : Avoid the center at all cost, or you will be killed !

Let's put all this data in a more convinient way to see.

Who is the prey ? Who is the predator ?

Let me introduce you : The Chess food chain :

Kings ♔ and Queens ♕ are the super-predator in this food chain as expected. Because Kings ♔ can't be captured, and Queens ♕ are the most powerfull pieces of the game.

Rooks ♖, Bishops ♗ and Knights ♘ are the middle-class predator. They are both preys and predators. It is interesting to note that Bishops ♗ and Knights ♘ have a special relationship : they tend to eat each other as well. Rooks ♖ are a special case mostly because they are used late-game.

We see that Pawns ♙ are at the bottom of the food chain. For every pieces, Pawns ♙ are the main component of their diet. They are resilient (I remind you that the odds of survival of a Pawns ♙ is the highest among the pieces) because they are numerous.

In conclusion

If the game have just started, take the Rook and hope for an quick mate. Because their death probability spike arrive later in the game.

If you arrive mid-game, as Ron in the film, take the Knight or the Bishop. It may seems counter-intuitive, but if a Knight manage to survive the early-game, then, it has lesser chance to die later, all things equals otherwise.

As a rule of thumb : Avoid the center !!! I am serious, you might not win the game, but you will stay alive !

Also, avoid to face foes that love to eat your piece type. So if you are a Pawn, just stay hidden for the whole game because everyone can kill you easly.

We have seen that Pawns are the true victims. What happen if we give them some weapons to defend themselves ?

Knights would be slaughtered again...
